Welcome to allseasons

Find out everything you ever wanted to know about seasons!

Broadly speaking, a season is a period of the year marked by changes in weather. Seasons exist because of the tilt in the Earth's axis.

A set of seasons is grouped into a calendar. Seasons are also reversed in the northern and southern hemispheres, while "traditional" seasons of summer, autumn, winter and spring may be quite meaningless to those living near the Equator.

Even these "traditional" seasons are not so simple:

  • Meteorological seasons are defined in line with the Gregorian calendar (ie the calendar as we know it).
  • Astronomical seasons are defined according to the equinoxes which relate to the Earth's elliptical orbit around the sun. These dates vary a little from year to year, but the seasons largely overlap with those of meteorological seasons

Many cultures have developed their own calendars, too. In south Asia the Hindu, Bengali and Tamil calendars recognise six seasons of two months each. In Australia, there are different calendars of indigeneous seasons recognised by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people.
